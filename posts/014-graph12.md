**Bound for classical Ramsey numbers**<br>
(Erdős) $R(p,p) > 2^{p/2}$ for $p \ge 3$.<br>
(Erdős, Szekeres) $R(p+1,q+1) \le \binom{p+q}{p}$<br>
(Graham and Rödl) $R(p+1,q+1) \le \frac{\binom{p+q}{p}}{\log\log(p+q)}$<br>
(Conlon) $R(p+1,q+1) \le p^{-c \log p / \log\log p} \binom{2p}{p}$<br>
(Sah) $R(p+1,q+1) \le e^{-c(\log p)^2} \binom{2p}{p}$<br>
(4 people) $R(p,p) \le (4-\varepsilon)^p$ for some constant $\varepsilon$.

**General Form of Ramsey Theorem**<br>
For any two positive integers $r, k$ and $q_1, q_2, \dots, q_k \ge r$, there is $N = N(q_1, q_2, \dots, q_k)$ such that for any $n \ge N$, and any $k$-colorings of $[n]^{(r)}$, there exists some $i$ with $1 \le i \le k$ and a $q_i$-set $S_i \subseteq [n]$ such that $S_i^{(r)}$ are in color $i$.

$[n] = \{1, 2, \dots, n\}$, $S_i^{(r)}$ 代表 $S_i$ 的所有 $r$-子集。

(Schur) For any given integer $k$, there exists an $N$ such that if $n \ge N$, then for any $k$-colorings of $[n]$, there exist $x, y, z \in [n]$ of the same color such that $x+y=z$.
