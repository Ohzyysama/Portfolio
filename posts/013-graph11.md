**(Heawood)**<br>
Every loopless planar graph is 5-colorable.<br>
**(Thomassen)**<br>
Let G be near-triangulation whose outer face is bounded by a cycle C, and let x and y be consecutive vertices of C. Supppose that L: V -> 2^N is an assignment of lists of colors to the vertices of G such that:<br>
(1). |L(x)| = |L(y)| = 1, where L(x) ≠ L(y).<br>
(2). |L(v)| ≥ 3 for all v ∈ V(C)\\{x,y}.<br>
(3). |L(v)| ≥ 5 for all v ∈ V(G)\V(C).<br>
Then G is L-colorable.<br>
推论：Every loopless planar graph is 5-list-colorable and 5-colorable.<br>
**Ramsey Theory**<br>
For any two integers p, q ≥ 2, R(p,q) is finite, and R(p,q) ≤ R(p-1, q) + R(p, q-1) for p,q ≥ 3.<br>
Moreover, if R(p-1,q) and R(p,q-1) are both even, then R(p,q) < R(p-1,q) + R(p,q-1).<br>
R(p,2) = p, R(2,q) = q.
